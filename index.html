    Federated Learning for Large Vision Models on Decentralized Heterogeneous Data

    05/2024 â€“ 12/2024

    Project Leader

    Project Overview

    Innovated Directional Normalized Adapter (DNA) and FedDNA methods, a novel parameter-efficient fine-tuning approach for heterogeneous federated learning. Achieved up to 2% accuracy improvement under severe data heterogeneity, with faster convergence and stable training. Validated the method through extensive experiments and theoretical analysis, proving its effectiveness in privacy-preserving federated settings.

    My Contribution
    • Problem Analysis: Identified performance degradation in federated learning due to label shift in client data distributions, particularly severe in skewed label scenarios. Recognized limitations of simple FedAvg method in handling imbalanced data distributions and local model forgetting issues.
    • Algorithm Design (PEFT): Improved LoRA efficient fine-tuning method by proposing DNA, decomposing weight updates into magnitude and direction components. Developed FedDNA to address local forgetting by introducing Key Direction constraints for better global-local alignment.
    • Experimental Validation: Conducted experiments using pre-trained ViT models on medical image datasets (Retina and Dermatology). Demonstrated superior classification accuracy of DNA in heterogeneous scenarios and faster convergence of FedDNA with improved stability.

    Project Overview

    In this work, we propose AIDoctor, a comprehensive medical chatbot that supports multi-modal queries, accurate online auscultation, and a user-friendly interface. We implemented Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) in AIDoctor, enabling language models to leverage high-quality textbook data. Specifically, AIDoctor is designed for three needs: i) data efficiency, ii) model integration, and iii) user-friendliness.

    My Contribution

    • Medical Dataset Collection and Processing (Python Crawler + Data Cleaning): Collected and organized datasets from real doctor-patient dialogues, AI-generated scenarios, and authoritative medical textbooks, resulting in a comprehensive dataset including high-quality medical knowledge bases.

Implemented KeyBERT for extracting highly relevant keywords and Phi-1.5 for follow-up question prediction.
